# Upwork Loom Script

The standardised Loom video Pang records and attaches to every Upwork application. Replaces per-job sales-script generation — same shape, different specifics, every time.

Target length: **~2:30** (under 3:00 always).
Voice rules baked in: no "co-founder" / "DivineSide" / "agency" framing; AIOS shown not defined; no code on screen; no live whiteboarding.

---

## [0:00–0:25] Open + plant

> Hey [Name], thanks so much for taking the time to watch this. I saw your post on Upwork — you're looking for someone to build [their thing] — and I've actually done something pretty close to this in the past for another client of mine, and I've worked with [their niche] companies before too. So I'm pretty confident I can do a great job on this for you.
>
> Quick thing before I dive in — like I mentioned in the proposal, I'm not really a one-off automation builder. I'm trying to work with you for the long term, and I think it's easier to *show* you what I mean than to explain it. So here's two things: first, a demo of the exact kind of system I built for another client that's basically your use case, and then I'll show you what I actually mean by 'building an AI Operating System for your business.'

---

## [0:25–1:20] Demo the past client's workflow (their use case)

> OK so this is the [system name] I built for [past client]. They had basically the same problem you're describing — [their problem]. Watch what happens when [trigger]: it [step 1]. Then [step 2]. Then [step 3]. The whole thing is running on its own. They're not clicking anything; the system handles it end-to-end.
>
> A couple things I want you to notice: first, it's making decisions, not just executing — [point at decision moment]. Second, it's working in their voice — when it sends [email / message / whatever], it sounds like them, because it has their context.

**Demo discipline:**
- Don't say "automation" / "workflow" / "bot" while narrating. Say "system" / "OS" / "running by itself".
- Show 2–3 distinct things happening that would normally need a human.
- Pause once to point at a decision moment ("notice it picked X over Y here — that's because…").

---

## [1:20–2:10] Zoom out — folder structure + context explanation

> Now here's the part that actually matters. This [demo system] is just one piece. The way I'd structure things for you is that everything sits inside a bigger system, organized by department.
>
> *[Switch view: show DivineCore Discord channels OR a folder tree — branding, sales, ops, support, etc.]*
>
> Each of these is a department of the business — sales, ops, branding, support — and each one runs its own functions. But they all share one thing: a **context folder**.
>
> *[Open the context folder, scroll through filenames.]*
>
> This is the most important part. Everything the system needs to know to make good decisions about your business lives here — your strategy, your customers, your competitors, your offer, your tone of voice, your pricing, your past deals. Everything you'd tell a great employee on day one.
>
> So when the ad-management system is making a decision about a campaign, it already knows your strategy, who you're targeting, who your competitors are, what you charge. It doesn't ask you. It decides the way you would. Same with lead qualification, same with email follow-up, same with support — they all pull from the same context.
>
> **That's what an AI Operating System actually is. It's basically a copy of your business that can run itself — your context, your decisions, your way of doing things — alongside you. You step in for the calls only you can make.**

---

## [2:10–2:30] Close

> Anyway, I've put a more detailed plan in the Google Doc attached to the proposal. If it looks like a fit, just reply on Upwork and we'll set up a call where I walk through this whole setup live and we sketch what yours would look like.
>
> Thanks again for watching.

## Simple-terms AIOS one-liners

Drop in if asked "wait, what's an AI Operating System?" or if [1:20–2:10] needs a tighter version:

1. **"It's a copy of your business that can run itself."**
2. **"It's hiring an employee who already knows everything about your business — your customers, your strategy, your tone, your competitors — and never forgets."**
3. **"It's a system that runs your business with you because it knows your business as well as you do."**

Pang's preferred order: lead with #1, immediately unpack with #2. That's what's woven into the [1:20–2:10] script above.

---

## Voice rules (do not violate)

- No "co-founder" / "DivineSide" / "agency". Solo-builder framing only.
- No "automation" / "workflow" / "bot" when describing the past client's system. Always "system" / "OS" / "running by itself".
- Don't define AIOS abstractly upfront. Show the demo, then the context folder, *then* land the definition.
- No code on screen.
- No live whiteboarding or hand-drawn diagrams. If a visual is needed, pre-record it and screenshare.
