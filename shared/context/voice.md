## Tone descriptors

The shape we're aiming for:

- **Direct, not preachy.** State the point. Don't soften it with disclaimers.
- **Confident, not arrogant.** "Here's what's true" beats "I think maybe possibly."
- **Operator-to-operator.** Written for people who do the work, not people who read about the work.
- **Concrete over abstract.** Specific examples, real numbers, named tools. "We built a Celery + Redis pipeline" beats "we leveraged orchestration infrastructure."
- **Short paragraphs.** Two or three sentences. Whitespace is a tool.
- **Plain English.** Cut "leverage," "synergy," "robust," "scalable" — every consultant uses them.

## DO

- Open with a sharp claim or a vivid scene. Earn the next line.
- Name specific people, tools, numbers, dates. Specificity is credibility.
- Show the mistake before the lesson. Receipts make it real.
- End with something the reader can act on, or something that reframes how they think.
- Use line breaks to control pacing.

## DON'T

- Don't open with "In today's fast-paced world..." or any variant.
- Don't list "5 ways to..." unless you actually have 5 distinct ways. Faking structure is obvious.
- Don't end with "What do you think? Drop a comment 👇" — that reads like a content bot.
- Don't use emoji as decoration. Use them as punctuation, sparingly, or not at all.
- Don't humble-brag. State results plainly.
- Don't write the word "leverage" as a verb. Ever.

## Banned phrases

| Banned | Why | Use instead |
|--------|-----|-------------|
| "leverage" (verb) | Consultant tell | "use" |
| "synergy" | Empty word | the actual mechanism |
| "robust" / "scalable" | Tech-bro filler | the actual property (handles X concurrent Y) |
| "in today's [fast-paced/digital/AI-driven] world" | Cliché opening | a concrete claim |
| "game-changer" | Hype | what it actually changes |
| "unlock" | LinkedIn cliché | the literal verb |
| "transform your business" | Empty promise | the specific outcome |
| "I'm excited to announce" | Hollow opener | the news, plain |

## Formatting preferences

### LinkedIn
- Hook in line 1. Earned curiosity, not clickbait.
- Line breaks between sentences for the first 3 lines (mobile cutoff).
- 100–250 words for posts. Longer when there's a story; never longer than the story warrants.
- Hashtags: 0–3, not 15. Or none.
- No emoji bullet points (✅ ❌). Use words.

> **For Pang's LinkedIn specifically**: the full operating manual lives in [`linkedin-playbook.md`](linkedin-playbook.md) — hooks, CTAs, closings swipe files; the 3 frameworks (PAS, SLA, Case Study); writing system flow; profile copy; ICP-narrowed audience (e-comm beauty brands, 10–50 employees, $500k+ revenue). Any agent generating a Pang LinkedIn post should load that file in addition to this one.

### YouTube (long-form)
- First 30 seconds = the hook. Tell them what they're getting and why.
- Show, don't tell. Whenever possible, demonstrate on screen instead of describing.
- Cold open the takeaway when the video allows. Then unpack.

### YouTube (Shorts) / Instagram Reels
- 0–3 second hook is everything.
- One idea per Short. If there are two, cut one.

### Email (sales, outbound)
- Subject line: under 8 words. Specific, not curious.
- First line: not "Hope you're well." Skip the warm-up.
- Under 100 words for cold; under 200 for follow-up.
- One ask. Never two.

### Email (newsletter, marketing)
- Personal opening — what's been going on, what we noticed, what we built. Like a letter, not a broadcast.
- One main idea per send.
- A single CTA at the bottom — and only when it earns its place.
