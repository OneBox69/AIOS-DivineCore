## 01 · The X Flywheel

X growth is not the goal. Followers are not the asset. **Leverage is the asset.**

The loop:

```
X Content → Profile → Email → Offer → Proof → Back to X
```

Every stage has to convert to the next, or the stage upstream is wasted. Audit the loop — not the tweet — when growth stalls.

| Stage | What should happen | What actually happens (2026-05-10) |
|-------|-------------------|------------------------------------|
| **X Content** | Posts attract operators / founders who already know AI is infrastructure (per [`audience.md`](../identity/audience.md)) | Not started — no posts yet |
| **Profile** | A first-time visitor immediately understands what DivineSide does and wants to subscribe | Not started — bio not finalised (see §10) |
| **Email** | The newsletter converts X attention into a longer relationship — one defined next action per send | Not started — newsletter doesn't exist |
| **Offer** | The custom AI OS pilot is tied directly to themes posted about that week | Not connected — no link from X to the offer |
| **Proof** | Client wins, build screenshots, weekly retainer numbers get told back on X — restarting the loop | Not started — wins aren't being told back on X |

> Whole loop is at zero today. Revisit this table monthly once posting starts — the right column is the diagnostic.

> If you're stuck under 10K followers, you don't need better tweets. You need a clearer loop. Identify the break before posting more.

## 03 · Relationship vs. Audience Filter

X is a tool to put Pang in specific rooms. Followers are a side effect of being in the right rooms.

**The 5 buckets of people we want X to put us in rooms with.** Specific names per bucket are TBD — fill in as you encounter the right accounts.

1. **ICP — specific e-comm beauty operators.** Named founders / COOs / ops managers at 10–50 employee, $500k+ brands you'd want to deliver a pilot for. *Specific names:* TBD.
2. **Peer AI-agency / build-in-public founders.** People building AI agencies, automation studios, or agentic products in public — audience overlap with Pang's. Mutual amplification compounds. *Specific names:* TBD.
3. **Category-defining voices.** People who already frame AI as infrastructure, not as a tool. If they quote-tweet, the AIOS framing lands harder on the rest of the timeline. *Specific names:* TBD.
4. **Top experts on the sales / marketing / content side.** Operators who teach the craft — sales systems, marketing systems, content systems. **Not** podcasters or journalists. *Specific names:* TBD.
5. **Aspirational mentors.** Long-game category-builders. People you want to become peers with, not just be retweeted by. *Examples Pang named:* Alex Hormozi, James Clear, Naval Ravikant.

Two checks before posting:

- Does this content attract those 5 buckets? — Yes / No
- Would they understand what DivineSide does from the profile alone? — Yes / No

If either is **no**, fix the upstream problem before posting more.

## 05 · Goal of the Threads

Every thread should serve at least two of these:

- **Drive ideal-fit founders to a DivineSide pilot conversation.** ICP in [`audience.md`](../identity/audience.md). Not chatbot buyers — operators who already know AI is infrastructure.
- **Establish category ownership.** Build language around the outcome we sell — *"your business, running on AI"* (per [`offer.md`](../sales-and-delivery/offer.md)). Internally we call it AI OS; externally we lead with the outcome, then earn the term.
- **High authority + likability.** Position the founders as people the reader wants to learn from. Aspirational without ego. Specific wins, not generic flexes.
- **Journey kick-off.** Leave the reader feeling they have to subscribe to follow what we're building.
- **Highly referenced.** Threads that get screenshotted, quoted, and shared inside founder circles. Build language other founders adopt.

## 06 · X Writing Guidelines

(Persona-level writing rules — Pang's tone, sentence length, content pillars, what he never writes — live in [`pang.md`](../identity/pang.md). The rules below are X-platform-specific extensions on top.)

**Structure**

- Thread headers (the first line of each tweet in a thread) are titles. Capitalize appropriately. No trailing punctuation.
- Use spacing consistently — single blank line between sentences and bullets. Never run-on paragraphs.
- Do **not** number the tweets in a thread. Numbered tweets get retweeted less.
- End each tweet with a **banger** — a sentence punchy enough to stand alone and be screenshotted.
- End the whole thread with a **bar** — one viral, hard-hitting line worth screenshotting on its own.
- Tag 4–6 relevant people / accounts per thread so they see, engage, share.

**Substance**

- Highly specific and actionable. No fluff. No "AI is changing everything."
- Lean into HOW and WHAT to do — actually share the system, the prompt, the agenda, the metric.
- Reader should feel they can't get this info anywhere else. If a sentence sounds like ChatGPT wrote it, rewrite.
- Give samples and examples wherever possible — sample agendas, real numbers, real prompts, real screenshots.
- Spell out thousands: **"$620,000 per month"** not "$620K per month".

**CTAs**

- Add the newsletter link or a follow ask at the end of every long-form thread (once the newsletter exists).
- Don't bury the CTA. Don't lead with it either.

**Inclusivity**

- When listing founders, operators, or examples, mix genders. Don't default to all-male lists.

**Sourcing**

- Lean on the founders' own work — meeting transcripts (Fathom), build screenshots, in-flight client wins. Cult following comes from authenticity, not curation.

**Differentiation Requirement**

Every X thread (and most single-tweet posts about the build) must make at least one explicit distinction between what DivineSide builds and what generic "AI automation" or "AI agency" players build. Without it, the thread gets bucketed with the rest of the automation timeline and the brand forfeits the category-ownership goal in §05.

**Why:** The X audience surfaces build detail naturally — peer founders care about how things are built. But *"I shipped a workflow that does X"* reads the same whether it's from us or from someone selling Zapier zaps. The difference has to be visible inside the thread, not just inferred from the bio. This is also the rule that protects against the "this just sounds like every other AI automation thread" failure mode.

**How to apply.** Pick at least one of these per thread — not all four, but at least one, surfaced clearly enough that a skimmer catches it:

- **The context layer.** We don't run AI on a one-off prompt. We build a context folder *with* the founder — markdown files covering their customers, business, strategy, voice, competitors, team, SOPs, lead channels. Every workflow reads from this folder before it makes a decision. The AI knows the business; it isn't guessing. (Source: [`offer.md`](../sales-and-delivery/offer.md) §02 Phase 1)
- **Embedded expertise.** We feed each workflow the public frameworks of domain experts — media buyers, copywriters, retention strategists, whoever owns the discipline that workflow lives in — so the AI makes decisions the way an expert would, not the way a generic chatbot would. (Source: [`../../CLAUDE.md`](../../../CLAUDE.md) §5)
- **Operating system, not standalone workflow.** We build a layer that wraps the entire business. Each new workflow compounds on the brain we built for the previous one — workflow #2 starts smarter than workflow #1, #3 starts smarter than #2. Standalone automations don't compound. (Source: [`offer.md`](../sales-and-delivery/offer.md) §07)
- **Skin-in-the-game guarantee.** 0% upfront, 100% on delivery. If we miss the target (functions delivered by week 8, or 10 hrs/week saved by month 2 when applicable), you don't pay anything and I pay you $500 out of pocket. Less load-bearing on X than on LinkedIn (the pilot offer is sold elsewhere per §07 below) but valid when a thread overlaps with the buyer narrative. (Source: [`offer.md`](../sales-and-delivery/offer.md) §04 and [`guarantee.md`](../sales-and-delivery/guarantee.md))

**Naming discipline still applies.** See §08 below and [`offer.md`](../sales-and-delivery/offer.md) §01: default to leading with the outcome (*"your business, running on AI"*) rather than the category noun (*"AI Operating System"*). Exception: foundational/announcement threads where the category itself is the subject — those earn the term by being the introduction.

## 07 · X Growth Strategy

(LinkedIn growth strategy — connection requests, "after 60 days" message, ICP outbound — lives in [`linkedin-playbook.md`](linkedin-playbook.md) §09. The X strategy below is **different in intent**: LinkedIn = client outreach inside the e-comm beauty ICP. X = peer community inside the automation / AIOS / build-in-public niche, where the 5 buckets in §03 live.)

### Reply Strategy

- Engage with accounts in your niche — people building in AI / automation / AIOS / agency-as-a-service.
- Engage with accounts you genuinely resonate with. **If you don't have something to say, don't force a reply.**
- No spam — every reply should be substantive. A bad reply costs more than no reply.
- The accounts you engage with regularly start engaging with your posts. Follow them; they tend to follow back.
- This compounds — a known route to 1k+ followers without ever cold-DMing.

### Posting

- **Your profile is a magnet.** It should clearly attract some people *and clearly repel others.*
- When you try to attract everyone, you attract no one.
- Post every day. Cadence beats perfection — pick the cadence and hold it.
- Keep the personality consistent so the magnet works the same way every visit.

### Retention — Keep them following once they do

1. **Keep giving them what they followed for.** If they followed for AIOS build threads, don't pivot to lifestyle takes.
2. **Show positive results from following your advice.** Receipts. Wins, before/after, screenshots, build moments.
3. **Personality is the secret sauce.** Lean into the quirks, interests, and unexpected angles. (See [`pang.md`](../identity/pang.md) — the 19yo Malaysian CS-student-building-in-public framing is the differentiator. Use it.)

### Outbound DMs

- DM operators with **under 1k followers** in the niche (automation / AIOS / agency builders / build-in-public devs).
- The under-1k threshold matters — they reply, they remember you, and they're future peers (the bucket-2 people in §03 often come from this pool over time).
- Goal is peer connection, not lead gen. The pilot offer is sold elsewhere (LinkedIn DMs / Upwork / Instantly).

**Prospecting — how to find them**

- Search a niche keyword in X's **People** tab (e.g. *"AI OS"*, *"automation"*, *"AI agency"*, *"agentic"*, *"build in public"*). Scroll until you see accounts whose bio signals the right niche AND follower count is under 1k.
- Skip accounts already followed by 10k+ peers — they get spammed daily and won't reply.
- Save the good ones to a list. Engage with their content for a week before DMing — replies first, message second.

**DM script (template — adapt the niche keyword)**

> Hey [name], how are you? I came across your profile and noticed that you [work with / build / are in] [shared niche — e.g. AI agencies, agentic systems, AI automation] too, so thought I'd reach out and connect 🤝 how long have you been [in this niche / building / using X] for?

The four parts of the script — keep all four, in this order:

1. **Warm greeting.** "Hey [name], how are you?" Low-friction opener, signals human not bot.
2. **Specific commonality.** Cite something visible on their profile — the tool they use, the niche they build in, the project they shipped. Generic = ignored.
3. **Soft handshake.** *"thought I'd reach out and connect"* — explicitly signals no pitch is coming.
4. **Open question on the commonality.** Gets a reply because it's specific and easy to answer. *"How long have you been..."* works because it triggers a small story, not a yes/no.

> Adapted from a reply-driving X DM template (Hamza, Framer niche, 2023). The structure transfers — only the niche keyword changes.

**Don't**

- Don't lead with what you're building or selling.
- Don't pitch on the first message — even a soft pitch.
- Don't ask "want to hop on a call" before the second exchange.

## 08 · Naughty Word List

(Canonical brand-level banned-phrases list lives in [`voice.md`](../identity/voice.md). X-specific additions on top.)

**Never**

- Don't say "Twitter" — it's "X"
- Don't number tweets in threads
- No swearing
- No mentions of cannabis, weed, drugs, alcohol, COVID, coronavirus
- Don't talk about engagement groups or paid amplification — even ironically
- Don't pitch "chatbots", "AI agents" generically, or "automations" as the offer — that's not what DivineSide sells

**Spelling and casing (DivineSide-specific)**

- **"AI Operating System"** or **"AI OS"** — never "ai operating system" or "AIOS" outside informal / internal contexts. **Public framing leads with the outcome** (*"your business, running on AI"*) per [`offer.md`](../sales-and-delivery/offer.md), not the category name.
- **"DivineSide"** — one word, two capitals. Never "Divine Side" or "divineside".
- **"founders"** or **"operators"** — not "entrepreneurs" (too broad), not "CEOs" (too corporate).
- **"A-players"** — not "A-Players".

## 09 · Ideal Audience Member — Dream Week

Anchor every thread to who you're writing for. Reference [`audience.md`](../identity/audience.md) for the full ICP definition. The X-specific lens:

The reader we want is a founder/operator who:

- Already runs a real business doing 7–8 figures, or is on a credible path to it
- Suspects most "AI tools" are noise but knows AI itself is infrastructure
- Reads about systems, not motivation
- Would rather see a build screenshot than a quote graphic
- Will trade their email for one specific, useful prompt or system breakdown
- Wants to be the person whose business runs without them — not the person who never has to work

**Their dream week**: business compounds without them touching it daily. Calendar runs lean. They ship things they actually want to ship. Customers are people they'd be happy to bump into. They unplug for stretches and the system keeps running. Travel is normal, not a reward.

Every thread should feel like it was written *for* this person, not at the timeline at large.
