## 01 · The headline promise

**"Your business, running on AI."**

Long-form, what we say on calls, in the audit doc, in the proposal:

> *"In 6 weeks, we build the AI brain that learns your business and runs the repetitive work — so your team gets at least 8 hours a week back to focus on growth. If we don't deliver that, you don't pay the second half."*

This promise is fixed. Don't soften it. Don't switch the metric to suit a specific workflow. Don't add caveats.

### Naming discipline

- **External / customer-facing:** *"Your business, running on AI."* Never lead with abstract category nouns ("AI Operating System," "AI Workforce," "Operating Brain") — they don't land with founders.
- **Internal / strategy / repo:** *AI Operating System.* DivineCore is the internal AI OS; every client build is an AI OS for that brand. CLAUDE.md uses this language; don't change internal vocabulary.
- **In-call frame** when a prospect asks *"what is this exactly?":* *"It's the AI brain for your business. It learns how you do things, then runs the repetitive work for you."* No abstract noun — just description and outcome.

## 02 · The pilot

**6 weeks, fixed shape.** What we build varies brand to brand. The shape doesn't.

### Phases

| Weeks | Phase | What happens |
|-------|-------|--------------|
| 1-2 | **Context** | We learn the business — products, voice, SOPs, decisions, why you do things the way you do. This becomes the AI brain. |
| 3-4 | **Data** | We connect the brain to the business — Shopify, Klaviyo, Gorgias, Meta, whatever's relevant. Now it sees what's happening in real time. |
| 5-6 | **Automate** | We ship one workflow that takes a specific repetitive task off the team's plate. Live, monitored, used daily. |

### Deliverables at the end of week 6

- The AI brain — a maintained context layer about the business
- One live workflow that demonstrably saves the team 8+ hours/week
- Documentation of what we built and how to extend it
- A 30-day support window where we tune the workflow as the team uses it

## 03 · Pricing

### Pilot

| Tier | Price | Available to |
|---|---|---|
| **Founding partner** | **£2.5k** | First 5 UK skincare brands |
| **Standard** | **£5k** | All brands after the first 5 |

Payment: 50% upfront, 50% at week 4 (subject to the guarantee — see §04).

### Retainer

**Foundation tier — £2k/month** (default post-pilot, opt-out, not opt-in)
- Context layer maintained as the business changes
- Monitoring + uptime on the live workflow(s)
- Weekly 30-min check-in
- Up to 2 active workflows included

**Each additional active workflow:** +£500/month.

Month-to-month. 30-day notice to cancel. No long contracts.

### Why £2.5k for the first 5

The first 5 pilots are co-development. We learn what works in skincare; you get the AI brain at half-price. The discount buys case studies, product learning, and templates future clients will pay full price for.

This is real money for them — enough to enforce scope discipline and ensure they actually use what we build. After 5 pilots, the price moves to £5k.

## 04 · The guarantee

**At least 8 hours per week back, by week 4. If we don't deliver, you don't pay the second half** (£1.25k for founding, £2.5k for standard).

### How we measure

- **Baseline (week 1):** the team logs the function we're automating — who does it, how often, how long. That log becomes the baseline.
- **Measurement (week 4):** same log, after the workflow is live. Difference = hours saved per week.
- **Verification:** we share the baseline and the post-launch log together. If it's not at 8+ hrs/week, the second-half invoice doesn't go out.

### One universal metric — hours saved

We don't switch to "response time" or "throughput" or "revenue impact" depending on the workflow. Those are useful secondary signals — track them, report them — but the *guarantee* is always hours.

Why hours: works for any workflow type, compounds visibly across pilots, easier to deliver than revenue impact, matches the existing case study (Reliable Medicare, 10+ hrs/week).

## 05 · Scope envelope

The pilot is **fixed in shape, variable in subject.** Inside the envelope:

- **One workflow per pilot.** Not "1-2." One.
- **Buildable in ≤2 weeks of engineering.** If a workflow needs more, it doesn't fit the pilot — it becomes a separate engagement.
- **Uses APIs we can access.** Standard ecom stack (Shopify, Klaviyo, Gorgias, Meta, Google, OpenAI/Anthropic) is fine. Niche internal tools are case-by-case.
- **Measurable in hours saved.** No "make our brand stronger" workflows.

Anything outside this envelope = separate engagement, separately priced. Don't wedge it into the pilot.

## 06 · What needs its own engagement

Real and valuable, but **not** in the £2.5k / £5k pilot:

- More than one workflow at a time (each additional = its own scope + price)
- Workflows requiring custom hardware or physical logistics
- Workflows requiring data we don't have API/permissions for
- Migrations from old tools or large-scale data clean-up
- Training programs or hiring support for client staff
- Anything taking more than 2 weeks of engineering

If a prospect wants any of these, scope and price separately in the audit doc. Don't pretend they fit.

## 07 · Expansion path — after the pilot

The pilot proves one workflow. The retainer keeps it running and adds the next ones, one at a time.

**Order of expansion:**
1. Same brand, same department, **second workflow** (e.g., CS triage → CS draft responses)
2. Same brand, **second department** (e.g., marketing pilot → returns automation)
3. Same brand, **full operational coverage** (compounding across functions)

**One task at a time, one department at a time.** Don't sell breadth before depth. The brand should feel the win on workflow #1 before they're scoped for workflow #2.

This is also how the AI brain (context layer) actually compounds — each new workflow runs faster and cheaper than the last because the brain is already trained on the business. By workflow #4, *they* describe it as their AI operating system. You earn the platform language by showing the value, not by claiming it.
