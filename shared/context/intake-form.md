## The 10 questions

### 1. Brand basics
- **Question:** What's your brand name and website URL?
- **Type:** Short text (or two short-text fields)
- **Why we ask:** Anchor + audit reference.

### 2. Annual revenue range
- **Question:** Roughly what's your annual revenue range?
- **Type:** Multiple choice (single-select)
- **Options:**
  - Less than £500k
  - £500k – £1M
  - £1M – £3M
  - £3M – £10M
  - £10M+
- **Why we ask:** Sanity-check fit (we target £500k–£5M for now). Calibrates the kickoff conversation.

### 3. Team
- **Question:** How many people on your team, and what does each person own? (Quick list is fine — e.g., *"5 people: founder = strategy, ops manager = CS + returns, marketer = content + ads, 2 fulfilment."*)
- **Type:** Long text
- **Why we ask:** Maps directly to the brain's `operations.md`.

### 4. Tools / tech stack
- **Question:** Which of these do you currently use? (Tick all that apply.)
- **Type:** Multi-select
- **Options:**
  - Shopify
  - WooCommerce / Magento / other CMS
  - Klaviyo
  - Mailchimp / other ESP
  - Gorgias
  - Zendesk / Re:amaze / other helpdesk
  - Recharge / Bold / other subscriptions
  - Yotpo / Loox / Stamped (reviews)
  - Meta Ads
  - Google Ads
  - TikTok Ads
  - Notion
  - Airtable
  - Google Workspace
  - Other (please specify in the next free-text answer)
- **Why we ask:** Determines which APIs we'll need to access in Phase 0 + 2.

### 5. Top priorities this year
- **Question:** What are your top 3 priorities for the business in the next 12 months?
- **Type:** Long text
- **Why we ask:** Frames `strategy.md`. Tells us whether they're in growth, optimisation, or survival mode.

### 6. Biggest operational pain
- **Question:** What's the one operational function eating the most of your team's time right now? (CS, returns, content, influencer ops, ad management, fulfilment, finance, etc.)
- **Type:** Long text
- **Why we ask:** This is the workflow we'll likely scope. The discovery call surfaced this; the form gets it written down so the kickoff doesn't re-tread.

### 7. Who handles it
- **Question:** Who currently handles that work, and roughly how many hours per week between them?
- **Type:** Long text
- **Why we ask:** Baseline anchor for the 8-hours/week guarantee. Tells us who we're augmenting and what their cost is.

### 8. Tried before?
- **Question:** Have you tried solving this before — agency, tool, hire, or nothing yet? If yes, what happened?
- **Type:** Long text
- **Why we ask:** Tells us what's already failed (so we don't repeat it) and gives a budget anchor.

### 9. Top customer complaints
- **Question:** What are the top 3 things your customers complain about? (Pull from reviews, CS tickets, DMs.)
- **Type:** Long text
- **Why we ask:** Maps to `customer.md`. Often surfaces secondary workflows worth scoping after the pilot.

### 10. Why now?
- **Question:** Why are you doing this now? What changed recently — or what would change in 6 months if you didn't?
- **Type:** Long text
- **Why we ask:** Surfaces urgency. Tells us whether we're solving a real fire or a vague want.

## What we deliberately don't ask in the form

The form skips anything that needs conversation:

- **Voice** (banned phrases, signature phrases, how the founder actually speaks)
- **Strategic decisions and the reasoning behind them** (the `decisions.md` content)
- **Why they chose specific tools / manufacturers**
- **What they tried that didn't work, in real detail**
- **Anything where the answer needs to be drawn out, not typed**

These get pulled out in the kickoff call. Trying to capture them in a form gets you generic answers — *"premium and fun"*, *"we wanted the best quality"*. Useless.

## What this doc is, what it isn't

**It is:**
- The locked intake-form content for the first 5 pilots
- The source-of-truth for what gets asked async vs. live

**It isn't:**
- Final — first 5 pilots will tell us which questions produced useful answers and which got skipped or answered uselessly
- A replacement for the kickoff call — the form is ~30% of the brain's eventual content; the call is the other 70%

**Update cadence:** revise after every 3 pilots. If a question consistently gets skipped or produces useless answers, replace or remove it.
